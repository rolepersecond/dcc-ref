CLERIC

• HD: 1d8
• Weapon training: A cleric is trained in the weapons used by faithful followers of his god, as shown on page 32. Clerics may wear any armor and their spell checks are not hindered by its use.
• Spellcasting: Spell check; Roll 1d20 + Personality modifier + caster level.
• Turn unholy: A cleric wields his holy symbol to turn away abominations.
• Lay on hands: Clerics heal the faithful. By making a spell check, a cleric may lay on hands to heal damage to any living creature.

THIEF

• HD: 1d6
• Weapon training: blackjack, blowgun, crossbow,dagger, dart, garrote, longsword, short sword, sling, and staff. Thieves are careful in their choice of armor, as it affects the use of their skills
• Thieves’ Cant: Thieves speak a secret language called the cant known only to members of their class.
• Thieving skills: A thief learns certain skills that aid his illicit pursuits.
• Luck and Wits: Thieves survive on their luck and their wits, and the most successful thieves live a life of fortune on guts and intuition. For each point of Luck expended, he rolls one die and applies that modifier to his roll. The thief’s Luck score is restored each night by a number of points equal to his level. This process cannot take his Luck score past its natural maximum.

WARRIOR

• HD: 1d12
• Attack modifier: Unlike other classes, warriors do not receive a fixed attack modifier at each level. At 1st level, this is a d3. The warrior rolls this d3 on each attack roll and applies it to both his attack roll and his damage roll.
• Mighty Deed of Arms: Warriors earn their gold with pure physical prowess. Prior to any attack roll, a warrior can declare a Mighty Deed of Arms, or for short, a Deed. This Deed is a dramatic combat maneuver within the scope of the current combat. The warrior’s deed die determines the Deed’s success. This is the same die used for the warrior’s attack and damage modifier each round. If the deed die is a 3 or higher
• Critical hits: In combat, a warrior is most likely to score a critical hit and tends to get the most destructive effects when he does so. At 1st through 3rd level, a warrior scores a crit on any natural roll of 19-20.
• initiative: A warrior adds his class level to his initiative rolls.
• Luck: At first level, a warrior’s Luck modifier applies to attack rolls with one specific kind of weapon. This kind of weapon must be chosen at first level

WIZARD

• HD: 1d4
• Attack modifier: Unlike other classes, warriors do not receive a fixed attack modifier at each level. At 1st level, this is a d3. The warrior rolls this d3 on each attack roll and applies it to both his attack roll and his damage roll.
• Weapon training: A wizard is trained in the use of the dagger, longbow, long- sword, shortbow, short sword, and staff. Wizards rarely wear armor, as it hinders spellcasting.
• Magic: Magic is unknown, dangerous, and inhuman. Wizards cast spells by making a spell check. A wizard’s spell check is usually 1d20 + Intelligence modifier + caster level.
• Supernatural patrons: Wizards weave magic spells in consultation with powers from supernatural places and the outer planes. Demons and devils, angels, celestials, ghosts, outsiders, daevas, genies, elementals, Chaos Lords, spirits, elder gods, alien intelligences, and concepts foreign to mortal comprehension whisper secrets in exchange for favors best left unexplained.
• Luck: A wizard’s Luck modifier applies to rolls for corruption (see page 116) and mercurial magic (see page 111).
• Spellburn: Before rolling any spell check, a wizard may declare that he will attempt spellburn. In attempting spellburn, the wizard temporarily expends points of his Strength, Agility, or Stamina score to enhance his spell check. For every ability point he expends, the wizard adds +1 to his spell check. Ability scores lost in this way return as the wizard heals. Each day he does not attempt spellburn, he recovers 1 point of ability score.
• Regaining Spells by spellburn: A wizard may use spellburn to cast spells he has lost for the day. If a wizard expends a lost spell’s level in ability score points, he can cast the spell as if he still had it.

DWARF

• HD: 1d10
• Weapon training: Dwarves prefer to battle with a weapon and shield. A dwarf is trained in the use of these melee weapons: battleaxe, club, dagger, handaxe, longsword, mace, short sword, spear, two-handed sword, and warhammer. A dwarf is also trained in these missile fire weapons: crossbow, javelin, shortbow, and sling. Dwarves wear whatever armor they can afford.
• Attack modifier: Unlike other classes, warriors do not receive a fixed attack modifier at each level. At 1st level, this is a d3. The warrior rolls this d3 on each attack roll and applies it to both his attack roll and his damage roll.
• Mighty Deed of Arms: Warriors earn their gold with pure physical prowess. Prior to any attack roll, a warrior can declare a Mighty Deed of Arms, or for short, a Deed. This Deed is a dramatic combat maneuver within the scope of the current combat. The warrior’s deed die determines the Deed’s success. This is the same die used for the warrior’s attack and damage modifier each round. If the deed die is a 3 or higher
• Luck: At first level, a warrior’s Luck modifier applies to attack rolls with one specific kind of weapon. This kind of weapon must be chosen at first level
• Sword and board: Dwarves excel at fighting with a shield in one hand and a weapon in the other. When fighting with a shield, a dwarf always gains a shield bash as a second attack. his shield bash uses a d14 to hit (instead of a d20). The dwarf adds his deed die to this number, as with all attacks, and can attempt Mighty Deeds of Arms involving the shield as well as his weapon. The shield bash does 1d3 damage. Some dwarves customize their shields with spikes or sharp edges to do more damage, while others enchant their shields with unique powers.
• Infravision: A dwarf can see in the dark up to 60’.
• Slow: A dwarf has a base movement speed of 20’, as opposed to 30’ for humans.:
• Underground Skills: Long life beneath the ground trains dwarves to detect certain kinds of construction. When underground, dwarves receive a bonus to detect traps, slanting passages, shifting walls, and other new construction equal to their class level. Additionally, a dwarf can smell gold and gems (100'. Or 40' for single coins.)

HALFING

• HD: 1d6
• Weapon training: Halflings prefer to battle with a weapon in each hand. A halfling is trained in the use of the club, crossbow, dagger, handaxe, javelin, shortbow, short sword, sling, and staff. Halflings usually wear armor it’s much safer, you know.
• Two-weapon Fighting: Halflings are masters at two-weapon fighting. A halfling is always considered to have a minimum Agility of 16 when fighting with two weapons. when fighting with two weapons, a halfling scores a crit and automatic hit on any roll of a natural 16. The halfling fumbles only when both dice come up a 1.
• Infravision: Halflings dwell in pleasant homes carved from the sod beneath hills. As such, halflings can see in the dark up to 30’.
• Small size: Halflings are 2 to 4 feet tall, and the stoutest among them weighs no more than 70 pounds. This small size allows them to crawl into narrow passages and through tiny holes.
• Stealth: Halflings are quite good at sneaking around. They receive a bonus to sneaking silently and hiding in shadows depending on their class level, as shown on table 1-18.
• Good luck charm: Halflings are notoriously lucky. First, a halfling doubles the bonus of a Luck check. For every 1 point of Luck expended, a halfling gains a +2 to his roll. Second, unlike other classes, a halfling recovers lost Luck to a limited extent. The halfling’s Luck score is restored each night by a number of points equal to his level. Third, a halfling’s luck can rub off on those around him. The halfling can expend Luck to aid his allies.
